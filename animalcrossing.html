Resources.

ACNH requires various materials and objects in order to build or achieve certain goals. There are two main sources that you will need to familiarise yourself with, Rocks and Trees. Yes, you can hit them willy-nilly, but there's certain ways to maximise the useage of these resources.

Getting the most out of your rocks.

As most Animal Crossing players know, when you equip a shovel (or an axe), you can hit rocks several times to collect resources. Along with this, a random rock each day will drop bells.

The more you hit a rock quickly, the more you'll get out of it. There's a method to making sure you don't get knocked back in order to maximise your profits:
  • Make sure there is nothing on all sides of the rock you plan to hit. If weeds are growing around the rock, they will take up valuable space - blocking room for the resources you chip away.
  • Stand at one of the corners of the rock, then dig two holes to the side of your player so that the rock cannot move you away. The ideal way is to stand to the lower right of a rock, dig one hole south, and one hole east.
  • If you are having trouble angling your character correctly, you can walk towards the spot where the two holes converge, causing you to jump over. Jumping back will face you directly at the rock.
  • Mash the A button while holding either an Axe or Shovel to rapidly hit the rock up to 8 times. That's the most amount of resources you can get per rock, per day. This can equal up to 16,400 bells if you're striking the random money rock.

Turnips

Turnips in animal crossing follow a surprisingly complicated system and can be one of the fastest ways to make a lot of bells. when you know how to game the stalk market you will be making more and more bells every week you do it if you reinvest your profits

The basics.

Every sunday between 5:00 and 12:00, daisy mae will visit your town to sell turnips. You can buy as many turnips as you want, provided you have the funds, here are a few pointers you should follow

  • Check your prices twice per day. there are differant prices depending on the time. one price for the morning and one for the afternoon
  • Make sure to sell your turnips before sunday afternoon. this will be your last chance to sell.
  • Turnips turn rotten after one week. if they turn rotten they will not be able to be sold

Turnip Patterns

as the heading says. you always want to aim to sell at the highest price.

You will always be able to buy turnips between 90-110 bells

every week your turnip prices will follow a pattern, these are

  • Large Spike
  • Small Spike
  • Decreasing
  • True random

    • Patterns will change every week and the one you get is unique. Next week's pattern is based off the previous weeks.

Large Spike

A large spike pattern is where the price will start off low, but halfway through the week the price will be the highest and will rapidly drop on subsequent days except for the very last day, where prices will recover a little.

Small Spike

A small spike pattern has higher than normal prices in the second half of the week with roughly five seperate prices being able to make a profit.

Decreasing

This pattern starts with high prices and gradually drops, however the prices for this pattern drop the slowest compared to sharp spikes in the other patterns. this pattern is the most stable but wont make you a lot of profit.

Random

A completly random pattern where the price is... you guessed it. completly randomized. this is the most unpredictable.
